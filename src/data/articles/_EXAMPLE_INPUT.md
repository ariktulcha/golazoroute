## Getting to Hard Rock Stadium

### By Car
Hard Rock Stadium is located at 347 Don Shula Drive in Miami Gardens, about 15 miles north of downtown Miami. Parking lots open 4 hours before kickoff with rates around $40-60 per game. The stadium has 24,000 parking spaces spread across multiple lots.

Take I-95 to the Florida Turnpike (SR 91) north, exit at NW 199th Street, and follow signs to the stadium. Allow extra time on match days as traffic can be heavy.

### By Ride-Share
Uber and Lyft are the most convenient options. From South Beach, expect $25-40 and 30-40 minutes depending on traffic. From downtown Miami, it's $20-30 and 25-35 minutes.

Designated ride-share pickup zones are located at Lots 8 and 18. After the match, expect surge pricing and 15-20 minute wait times.

### By Public Transit
Miami-Dade Transit offers special event bus service on match days. Take the Metrorail Orange or Green Line to Brownsville station, then transfer to express buses going directly to the stadium ($2.25 each way).

Regular bus routes 9, 27, and 119 also serve the stadium area but are slower with more stops.

### From the Airport
Miami International Airport (MIA) is 20 miles south, about 30-40 minutes by car. Uber/Lyft costs $25-35. Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood Airport (FLL) is 25 miles north, 35-45 minutes and $30-40.

## What to Do in Miami

### Beaches
**South Beach** is the most famous with wide white sand, art deco architecture, and people watching. Arrive early (before 10am) to claim space.

**Key Biscayne** offers calmer waters and family-friendly atmosphere at Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park and Crandon Park Beach.

**Haulover Beach** features a clothing-optional section and excellent conditions for kiteboarding.

### Culture & Art
**Wynwood Walls** showcases massive murals by world-renowned street artists. Free to visit, best during the day or Thursday-Saturday evenings when galleries are open.

**Pérez Art Museum Miami (PAMM)** displays contemporary art with stunning Biscayne Bay views. $16 admission, free first Thursday of each month.

**Little Havana** immerses you in Cuban culture with cigar shops, domino games at Maximo Gomez Park, and authentic restaurants along Calle Ocho.

### Nightlife
**Miami Beach** offers mega-clubs like LIV at Fontainebleau and Story, with $20-40 covers and expensive drinks ($15-20).

**Wynwood** has craft breweries, dive bars, and casual vibes. Try Wynwood Brewing Company or Gramps.

**Brickell** features rooftop bars with skyline views like Sugar at EAST and Area 31 at Kimpton EPIC.

### Everglades
Take an airboat tour through this unique ecosystem just 45 minutes west of Miami. See alligators, birds, and subtropical wilderness. Tours range from $25-60.

## FAQ

**How far is Hard Rock Stadium from South Beach?**
About 20 miles or 30-40 minutes by car depending on traffic. Uber costs $25-40. There's no direct public transit, so plan for transportation.

**What's the best area to stay in Miami for World Cup?**
South Beach offers the best Miami experience with beaches and nightlife, but it's pricey and 30 minutes from the stadium. Miami Beach Mid-Beach provides good balance of beach access and value. Downtown Miami is most affordable and central.

**Is Hard Rock Stadium indoor or outdoor?**
It's open-air with a partial canopy roof covering all seats. The sides are open, creating natural airflow while protecting fans from sun and rain. Expect hot, humid conditions.

**Can I visit the Everglades during my trip?**
Yes! The Everglades are just 45 minutes west of Miami. Book a morning airboat tour ($25-60) and return to the city by afternoon. Many tour operators offer hotel pickup.

**What should I pack for Miami in summer?**
Light, breathable clothing, swimwear, sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses, hat, comfortable walking shoes, and a light rain jacket for afternoon storms. Bring nice clothes for upscale restaurants and clubs.

**Is Miami Beach the same as South Beach?**
Miami Beach is the barrier island city. South Beach (SoBe) is the famous southern neighborhood of Miami Beach with art deco architecture and Ocean Drive. When people say "Miami Beach," they usually mean South Beach.

**How expensive is Miami compared to other US cities?**
Miami is expensive, comparable to Los Angeles or Chicago. Expect $15-25 for casual meals, $40-80 for nice dinners, $250-500 for South Beach hotels. Budget travelers can find deals in downtown or by eating at Latin restaurants.

**Do I need to speak Spanish?**
No, but it helps! Many Miami residents speak Spanish, especially in Little Havana, Hialeah, and some service industries. All tourist areas and attractions operate in English.

**What's the best way to get from the airport to South Beach?**
Uber/Lyft ($25-35, 20-30 minutes) is easiest. Miami Beach Airport Express bus ($2.75) is cheapest but takes 60+ minutes. Taxis cost $35-45. Car rental works if you plan to drive around.

**Should I rent a car in Miami?**
If staying only in South Beach and downtown, Uber/Lyft may suffice. For visiting multiple neighborhoods, the Everglades, or stadium on game day, a car provides more flexibility despite parking costs.
